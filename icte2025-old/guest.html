Prof. Qingquan Chen

Fellow, Chinese Academy of Engineering
Fellow, Royal Academy of Engineering (UK)
Honorary Fellow, Hungarian Academy of Engineering
Honorary Fellow, Academician of the Ukrainian Academy of Engineering Sciences
Honorary Fellow, Founding Academician of the UN World Academy of Digital Sciences
Honorary Professor, The University of Hong Kong
Distinguished Chair Professor, The Hong Kong Polytechnic University

Prof. Qingquan Chen is a world-renowned expert in electrical engineering and electric vehicles. He was elected Academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering in 1997, becoming the first Hong Kong-based scholar to receive this honor. He has since been elected to several prestigious academies, including Fellow of the Royal Academy of Engineering (UK), Honorary Fellow of the Hungarian Academy of Engineering, Academician of the Ukrainian Academy of Engineering Sciences, and Founding Academician of the UN World Academy of Digital Sciences.

Currently, he serves as Honorary Professor of The University of Hong Kong and Distinguished Chair Professor of The Hong Kong Polytechnic University. His previous positions include Visiting Professor at MIT, UC Berkeley, and Cambridge University, as well as technology advisor to governments and multinational corporations. He is the Founding and Rotating Chairman of the World Electric Vehicle Association and Founder of the International Academicians Innovation Center.

Professor Chen has received numerous honors, including the Prince Philip Medal from the Royal Academy of Engineering, the Guanghua Engineering Science and Technology Award from the Chinese Academy of Engineering, the Silver and Gold Bauhinia Stars from the Hong Kong SAR Government, and was named "Touching China" Person of the Year in 2022. He is widely recognized as the "Father of Asian Electric Vehicles" and served as a member of the 10th National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference.

His research focuses on electric vehicle technologies, smart energy systems, large-scale electrical systems, and the integration of human, information, and physical systems. He has authored more than 450 academic papers, 18 books, and holds 10 patents.

Four Networks and Four Flows and the Cultivation of Technical Talent

Prof. Yubin Lan

Fellow, European Academy of Sciences, Arts and Humanities
Foreign Member, Russian Academy of Natural Sciences
Foreign Member, Georgian National Academy of Sciences
Director and Chief Scientist, National Joint International Research Center for Aviation Application Technology in Precision Agriculture

Prof. Yubin Lan is a Doctoral Supervisor and National Specially-Appointed Expert. He holds several high-level talent titles, including the Ministry of Educatio's "Overseas Master", Leading Talent of Guangdong Province's "Pearl River Talent Plan", Beijing Specially-Appointed Expert, and Top Talent of Shandong Province's "Case-by-Case Negotiation" Program. He served as the Dean of the School of Electronic Engineering at South China Agricultural University and was the founding Dean of the School of Artificial Intelligence from June 2019 to May 2024. He is also the Director and Chief Scientist of the National Joint International Research Center for Aviation Application Technology in Precision Agriculture.

He currently holds key positions in several influential organizations, including Chairman of the UN International Telecommunication Union (ITU) and Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) Focus Group on Digital Agriculture, Chairman of the International Society for Precision Agriculture Aviation (CIGR), and Vice Chairman of the World UAV Federation. In China, he serves as Director of the Agricultural Aviation Branch of the Chinese Society of Agricultural Engineering, Executive Vice Chairman of the National Aviation Plant Protection Technology Innovation Alliance, and Chairman of the Academic Committee of the Key Laboratory for Agricultural Aviation Plant Protection under the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs.

He has received numerous prestigious awards, such as the First Prize of the 9th Contribution Award for Overseas Chinese, the First Prize of the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs Harvest Award, and the Guangdong Provincial Science and Technology Cooperation Award. His academic influence is demonstrated by his consistent inclusion in the Elsevier Highly Cited Chinese Researchers list (2022-2024) and the Stanford University Global Top 2% Scientists list for five consecutive years. His publications have accumulated over 12,000 citations in the past five years, with an H-index of 62.

He was elected Academician of the European Academy of Sciences, Arts and Humanities in 2018 and Foreign Academician of the Georgian Academy of Agricultural Sciences in 2019. In 2021, he was a valid candidate for Foreign Academician of the Chinese Academy of Engineering.

The Low-Altitude Economy and Talent Development

Mr. Mingtao Li

Chief E-commerce Expert, China International Electronic Commerce Center (CIECC), Ministry of Commerce

Mr. Mingtao Li is Chief E-commerce Expert at the China International Electronic Commerce Center (CIECC) under the Ministry of Commerce. With extensive experience in e-commerce policy and market research, he has contributed to the formulation of a range of national e-commerce policies and standards in areas such as E-commerce Demonstration Cities and Bases, E-commerce Demonstration Enterprises, E-commerce in Rural Areas, Cross-border E-commerce, and E-commerce Statistics.

He was honored as a Model Worker of the Ministry of Commerce in 2016. Mr. Li led the compilation of the Ministry's annual China E-commerce Report from 2013 to 2024 and served as a core expert in drafting China's 13th and 14th Five-Year Plans for e-commerce development.

He holds multiple advisory roles, including expert membership in the National Digital Trade Expert Group, the National E-commerce Demonstration City Expert Committee, the National E-commerce Standardization General Group and TC563, the APEC E-commerce Business Alliance Expert Committee, the China Information Industry Association, the Cross-border E-commerce Committee of the China Customs Society, and the E-commerce Professional Committee of the World Internet Conference. He also serves as an e-commerce consultant to several local governments.
