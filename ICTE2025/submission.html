Paper Submission

Submission of Abstract

When preparing your abstract, please follow the abstract guidelines.

Abstracts and papers should be written in English. All submissions will be reviewed for relevance, originality, clarity, soundness, and significance by at least two reviewers.

Authors with an abstract accepted by the Conference will be allocated a time-slot for presentation if they have paid the registration fee. Please note that no presentation time will be allocated until the author registration has been completed.

Please submit your abstract through the Paper Submission System: CMT Submission System

Submission of Final Abstract or Full Paper

Upon acceptance of the submitted abstract, authors must submit their final abstract or full papers by the stated deadline. To have their abstracts or full papers included in the e-Proceedings of the Conference, authors must follow the following procedure for submission.

1
Authors must complete the registration by the stated deadline.
2
Authors must address the points suggested by the reviewers (if any) in preparing the final abstract or full paper.
3
The abstract or full paper should be prepared in Microsoft Word, in accordance with the Springer's Computer Science Proceedings formatting guidelines. A sample paper is also available for your easy reference.
4
The abstract or full paper must have been properly proofread and checked for accuracy by the authors. The paper file in Microsoft Word format must be submitted through the Paper Submission System (https://cmt3.research.microsoft.com/ICTE2025/) by the stated deadline.

For a full paper, the standard length is 10--12 pages.

The Conference reserves the right to reject any paper submitted for publication in the e-Proceedings if it is considered to be below the required standard.

Submission of publication in Springer CCIS series (indexed by EI Compendex and Scopus)

Each full paper submitted to the Conference will undergo a second round of review for publication as a chapter in the edited volume of Springer's Communications in Computer and Information Science series. Please follow the proceedings guidelines. Any paper in excess of 12 pages will be subject to extra page charge at HK$200 per page up to a maximum of 15 pages.

In the previous year, selected papers of ICTE 2024 were published by Springer in CCIS: Vol. 2330.
