General Information

Shenzhen, China

Shenzhen, situated in southern Guangdong Province, is China's pioneering Special Economic Zone and a global innovation hub. This metropolis of 17 million people integrates cutting-edge technology with coastal landscapes, hosting 29,700 national high-tech enterprises including Huawei and Tencent headquarters. Key landmarks include the 599m Ping An Finance Center and UNESCO-designated Mangrove Nature Reserve.

City Highlights
  • Innovation Ecosystem: Generates 15.9% of China's PCT patents annually
  • Transportation Hub: Bao'an Airport (60m passengers/year) and 547km metro network
  • Cultural Attractions: Window of the World theme park and OCT-LOFT art district
Education System
  • 14 higher education institutions including Shenzhen University
  • Vocational education pioneers like Shenzhen City Polytechnic

Explore more: Shenzhen Tourism Official Site

Shenzhen City Polytechnic

Shenzhen City Polytechnic, established in 2024 through the transformation of Shenzhen Technician College, is a public higher vocational institution occupying 555 acres in Longgang District. The campus houses 11,000 students across 13 academic divisions.

Entry Requirements

All foreign visitors entering Shenzhen must comply with China's visa regulations. Key requirements include:

  • Valid passport with 6+ months remaining
  • Pre-approved visa (recommend applying 1-2 months in advance)

Check latest policies: National Immigration Administration

  • After arriving in China, purchase one at the airport or a service center.

Making payments in Shenzhen

Shenzhen uses the Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB) as legal tender. While cash remains a valid payment method, mobile payments dominate over 98% of transactions.

WeChat Pay and Alipay form a duopoly in mobile payments, collectively covering 93.3% of China's non-bank mobile payment market. Both systems support foreign card binding with 200+ country coverage.

  • Metro Line 3 - Alight at Huilongpu (回龙铺) Station, Exit C. Transfer to bus M446/M442 to Polytechnic Gate

Personal safety information

Shenzhen is a very safe city, you can find more information at Shenzhen - Wikitravel. The emergency phone number in China is 110 for police, 120 for ambulance, and 119 for fire. Calls can generally be made in Chinese or English.

Other matters

Electrical sockets in China are typically Type A, Type I, and Type C. The standard voltage is 220V, and the frequency is 50Hz. More information can be found at worldstandards.
